Matchmaker is a self-hosted Discord bot that brings competitive matchmaking to your server's custom games. It automatically divides players into balanced teams using a modified TrueSkill rating system, tracks wins and losses per guild, and maintains a persistent leaderboard — all through native Discord slash commands and interactive UI elements with no external website required.

## What Matchmaker does

Matchmaker manages the full lifecycle of a custom match — from recruiting players to recording results and updating ratings:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Players join a lobby">
    Use `/start` to open a lobby. Players click **Join** or **Leave** buttons directly in Discord to sign up for the next match.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Teams are balanced automatically">
    When the host clicks **Start Game**, Matchmaker evaluates player ratings and creates the most evenly matched teams possible, showing each team's predicted win probability.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Play the match">
    Players are optionally moved into separate Discord voice channels (Team A / Team B). The match embed stays visible throughout the game.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Record the result">
    Click **Record Result** to submit final scores. Matchmaker updates everyone's MMR using the modified TrueSkill algorithm, accounting for margin of victory.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Key features

- **Slash command integration** — `/start`, `/leaderboard`, and `/history` work natively in Discord without any external UI
- **Modified TrueSkill MMR** — Ratings account for score margin, not just win/loss, rewarding dominant performances
- **Flexible team sizes** — Supports any N v M format; teams don't have to be equal size
- **Rating decay** — Inactive players' uncertainty (σ) gradually increases, keeping the leaderboard accurate over time
- **Per-guild data** — Each Discord server maintains its own independent leaderboard and match history via a local SQLite database
- **Voice channel management** — Automatically moves players into team voice channels with the **Move** button
- **Match history and graphs** — View past matches and visualize rating progression with ASCII charts via the **Rating and History** user command
